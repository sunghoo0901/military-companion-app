# User Journey

> **Military Companion is designed to support soldiers throughout every stage of their military journey—from uncertainty before enlistment to confidence after discharge.**

---

# Purpose

Military Companion is not intended to solve a single problem.

It exists to support soldiers throughout an entire chapter of their lives.

The application should evolve alongside the soldier.

The guidance a new recruit needs is very different from the guidance someone preparing for discharge needs.

As soldiers grow, Military Companion should grow with them.

---

# Journey Overview

Military Companion supports five major stages.

```
Before Enlistment

↓

Early Military Service

↓

Mid Military Service

↓

Late Military Service

↓

After Military Service
```

Every stage has different goals, emotions, and opportunities.

---

# Stage 1 — Before Enlistment

## User Mindset

"I don't know what military life will be like."

Most future soldiers feel uncertain.

They are searching for answers, trying to prepare, and hoping they do not miss anything important.

---

## Primary Goals

Help future soldiers:

- Understand military life.
- Prepare before enlistment.
- Build confidence.
- Reduce anxiety.
- Know what to expect.

---

## Primary Features

- Military preparation guides
- Packing checklists
- Timeline overview
- Frequently asked questions
- AI Companion
- Preparation resources

---

## Emotional Journey

Anxious

↓

Prepared

---

# Stage 2 — Early Military Service

## User Mindset

"Everything is new."

The soldier is adjusting to military life.

Information overload is common.

Many soldiers worry about making mistakes or missing opportunities.

---

## Primary Goals

Help soldiers:

- Understand military systems.
- Learn military terminology.
- Track important milestones.
- Discover benefits.
- Build healthy habits.

---

## Primary Features

- Daily Focus
- Service timeline
- Leave tracking
- Promotion timeline
- Military benefits
- AI Companion

---

## Emotional Journey

Overwhelmed

↓

Relieved

↓

Confident

---

---

## Primary Features

- Certification guidance

- Leadership resources
- Goal tracking
- Personalized recommendations

---

## Emotional Journey

Comfortable

↓

Growing

↓

Motivated

---

# Stage 5 — After Military Service

## User Mindset

"My military service is over."

The Companion relationship should not end.

Military Companion should continue supporting users as they pursue the next chapter of their lives.

---

## Primary Goals

Support users as they:

- Begin university.
- Start careers.
- Build businesses.
- Continue learning.
- Achieve personal goals.

---

## Long-Term Vision

Military Companion should continue evolving into a lifelong personal growth platform.

Military service becomes the beginning of that relationship, not the end.

---

# The Conversation Never Ends

Military Companion should never feel like software.

It should feel like someone walking alongside the soldier.

The relationship begins with onboarding.

It continues through every milestone.

Examples:

Welcome.

↓

Let's get to know each other.

↓

I noticed you've been exploring certifications.

↓

You're halfway through your service.

↓

Let's start preparing for life after military service.

↓

Congratulations on your discharge.

↓

What's your next goal?

The application should remember previous interactions and become increasingly helpful over time.

---

# Progressive Personalization

Military Companion should not ask users dozens of questions during onboarding.

Instead, it learns gradually.

Examples include:

- Interests
- Future goals
- Frequently viewed topics
- Saved opportunities
- AI conversations
- Completed milestones

Every interaction helps the application better understand the soldier.

---

# The Companion's Role

Military Companion is not simply an information provider.

Its responsibilities include:

- Explaining
- Guiding
- Recommending
- Encouraging
- Celebrating
- Preparing

The Companion should proactively help soldiers rather than waiting for them to ask questions.

---

# Success

The journey succeeds when soldiers feel:

"I always knew what I should focus on."

"I never missed important opportunities."

"I became more prepared every month."

"I made the most of my military service."

Most importantly:

"I never had to figure it out alone."
