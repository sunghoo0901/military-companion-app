# Product

> **Military Companion is not a collection of features. It is a personalized companion that guides every soldier to get the most out of their military service.**

---

# Product Mission

Military Companion exists to guide every Korean soldier throughout their military service.

The application should reduce uncertainty, surface opportunities, and help soldiers confidently navigate one of the most important transitions in their lives.

Every feature should contribute toward one outcome:

> **Helping soldiers leave military service in a better position than when they entered.**

---

# Product Vision

Military Companion should become the trusted digital companion used by every Korean soldier.

Rather than functioning as a military information database, Military Companion should proactively guide users throughout their service by understanding:

- Who they are
- Where they are in their service
- What their goals are
- What opportunities are available

The product should continuously evolve alongside the soldier.

---

Our goal is not simply to provide information.

# The Core Problem

Military life contains countless opportunities.

However...

Many soldiers never discover them.

Examples include:

- Financial programs
- Military discounts
- Career opportunities
- Promotion preparation
- Leave optimization
- Certifications
- Scholarships
- Government benefits

Information exists.

But it is scattered.

Military Companion exists to organize that information into actionable guidance.

---

# Our Product Philosophy

Military Companion should answer one question every day.

> **"What should I do today?"**

Instead of requiring soldiers to search through menus, regulations, or websites, the application should proactively recommend the most valuable actions based on each user's current situation.

---

# The Opportunity Engine

At the heart of Military Companion is the Opportunity Engine.

The Opportunity Engine continuously evaluates:

- Service stage
- Branch
- Rank
- Goals
- Current date
- Upcoming milestones
- Available benefits

Then asks:

> **What opportunity should this soldier know about today?**

Examples include:

- Promotion registration
- New military discounts
- Financial opportunities
- Career events
- Certifications
- Leave recommendations
- Scholarships
- Government programs

Every recommendation should be personalized.

---

# Product Layers

Military Companion consists of three layers.

## Layer 1

Features

Examples:

- Leave
- Benefits
- Promotions
- Career
- Finance
- AI

These are tools.

---

## Layer 2

Guidance

The application organizes those tools into personalized recommendations.

Instead of simply displaying information, Military Companion explains:

- Why it matters
- When it matters
- What to do next

---

## Layer 3

Life Impact

The ultimate outcome is helping soldiers finish their service:

- More prepared
- More confident
- More informed
- More financially secure
- Better positioned for life after military

---

# Personalization

Every experience should adapt to the individual soldier.

Inputs include:

- Branch
- Rank
- Unit
- Enlistment date
- Expected discharge
- Goals
- Certifications
- Interests

Different soldiers should receive different recommendations.

No two home screens should be identical.

# Home Screen Philosophy

The Home screen is not a dashboard.

It is a personalized daily briefing.

Every time the user opens the application, they should immediately understand:

- What deserves attention today
- What opportunities are available
- What milestone is approaching
- What progress they have made

The Home screen should reduce decision fatigue.

---

# Design Philosophy

Military Companion should feel:

- Calm
- Clear
- Friendly
- Intelligent
- Reliable

Never:

- Overwhelming
- Bureaucratic
- Complicated
- Stressful

---

# Product Decision Filter

Before building any feature, ask:

Does this help soldiers get more out of their military service?

If the answer is no, reconsider building it.

If the answer is yes, determine whether it aligns with our mission and improves the overall journey.

---

# Success

Military Companion succeeds when soldiers say:

"I knew exactly what I needed to do."

"I didn't miss important opportunities."

"I felt prepared."

"I made the most of my military service."

Those outcomes matter more than downloads, page views, or feature counts.

---

# Long-Term Goal

Military Companion should become the operating system for military life.

It should be trusted enough that every soldier naturally opens the application whenever they need guidance, reassurance, or an answer.

The application should eventually become the official digital companion supported by the Ministry of National Defense.
