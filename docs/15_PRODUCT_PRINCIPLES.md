# Product Principles

> **These principles guide every product decision made within Military Companion.**

---

# Purpose

Military Companion exists to improve the lives of Korean soldiers.

Every feature, design decision, and roadmap discussion should align with these principles.

---

# 1. Solve Real Problems

We build products that solve validated user problems.

Interesting ideas are not enough.

---

# 2. Support the Soldier's Journey

The product should adapt to where each soldier is in their military service.

Context is more valuable than generic information.

---

# 3. Personalization Over Generalization

Whenever possible, recommendations should reflect the user's:

* Branch
* Rank
* Unit
* Service stage
* Goals

---

# 4. Guidance Over Information

Military Companion should not simply display information.

It should help users understand what that information means and what they should do next.

---

# 5. Trust Above Everything

Accuracy is essential.

If information is uncertain, outdated, or varies by unit, communicate that clearly.

Trust takes years to build and moments to lose.

---

# 6. Reduce Cognitive Load

Military life already involves information overload.

Present only what matters at the right time.

---

# 7. Celebrate Progress

Help soldiers recognize their achievements and milestones throughout their service.

Small wins contribute to long-term motivation.

---

# 8. Long-Term Thinking

Every feature should improve the overall journey—not just solve today's problem.

---

# 9. Validate Before Expanding

New features should be supported by research whenever possible.

Growth should follow evidence rather than assumptions.

---

# 10. Build for Everyone

Military Companion should remain accessible and intuitive regardless of a soldier's technical background.

---

# Final Principle

Our goal is not to build the most feature-rich military application.

Our goal is to build the most trusted companion a soldier can have during military service.
