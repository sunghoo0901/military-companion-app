# User Research

> **This document records real user problems, observations, hypotheses, and validation for Military Companion.**

---

# Purpose

Military Companion exists to solve real problems experienced by soldiers.

This document serves as the single source of truth for everything we learn from users.

Every major product decision should be supported by evidence collected here.

---

# Research Principles

We believe:

* We are not our users.
* Assumptions must be validated.
* Real experiences matter more than opinions.
* Problems are more valuable than feature requests.

Whenever possible, we ask:

> "What problem are you trying to solve?"

instead of

> "What feature do you want?"

---

# Founder Research

The following observations are based on my own military experience.

These are valuable starting points but should still be validated with additional soldiers.

## Problem 1 — Military Information is Scattered

### Observation

Important information exists across multiple locations including regulations, military websites, banks, notices, and word of mouth.

### Impact

Soldiers spend unnecessary time searching for information.

Many never find it.

---

## Problem 2 — Promotion Requirements Are Unclear

### Observation

I did not know what tests or qualifications were required for promotion.

### Impact

Soldiers may delay promotions simply because they lack information.

---

## Problem 3 — Leave Planning is Difficult

### Observation

I had to build my own spreadsheet to understand my military timeline and vacation planning.

### Impact

Planning requires unnecessary manual work.

---

## Problem 4 — Unit Differences Create Confusion

### Observation

Many policies and practices differ between units.

### Impact

Generic answers are often incomplete or misleading.

The application must clearly distinguish between official regulations and unit-specific practices.

---

## Problem 5 — Benefits Are Difficult to Discover

### Observation

Many soldiers do not know about discounts, financial benefits, or government programs available to them.

### Impact

Valuable opportunities are frequently missed.

---

## Problem 6 — Career Anxiety

### Observation

Many soldiers worry about what they will do after discharge but do not know where to begin preparing.

### Impact

Preparation often starts too late.

---

# Research Hypotheses

These are assumptions that require validation.

## Hypothesis 1

Most soldiers miss at least one valuable military benefit because they never learn about it.

**Status:** Unvalidated

---

## Hypothesis 2

A personalized military timeline reduces uncertainty.

**Status:** Supported by founder experience

Needs additional validation.

---

## Hypothesis 3

Soldiers prefer guidance based on their current stage of service rather than browsing information manually.

**Status:** Unvalidated

---

## Hypothesis 4

Career preparation is one of the highest-value features during the second half of military service.

**Status:** Unvalidated

---

# Future Research

Questions we want to answer:

* What information do soldiers search for most often?
* What opportunities are most commonly missed?
* Which benefits create the greatest value?
* Which military regulations are most confusing?
* What tasks consume the most unnecessary time?
* Which features would soldiers use every week?
