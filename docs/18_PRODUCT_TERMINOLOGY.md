# Product Terminology

> **Military Companion should speak one consistent language. Every word in the application contributes to the overall experience and brand.**

---

# Purpose

This document defines the official terminology used throughout Military Companion.

Consistency builds trust.

Whenever possible, use the terms defined here across:

- Product design
- UI
- Documentation
- Code
- Marketing
- Notifications
- AI responses

---

# Core Philosophy

Military Companion should communicate like a trusted companion.

Words should feel:

- Human
- Clear
- Friendly
- Professional
- Encouraging

Avoid unnecessary military jargon unless it improves clarity.

---

# People

## Soldier

Preferred.

Use:

- Soldier
- You

Avoid:

- User
- Customer
- Client

Military Companion exists for soldiers.

---

# Main Navigation

## Home

Preferred.

Not:

Dashboard

Reason:

Home feels welcoming.

Dashboard feels like business software.

---

## Journey

Preferred.

Represents the soldier's military journey.

Avoid:

- Timeline
- Progress Page

Journey communicates growth rather than simply tracking time.

---

## Benefits

Preferred.

Includes:

- Financial benefits
- Military discounts
- Government programs
- Travel benefits
- Banking
- Entertainment

---

## Career

Preferred.

Includes:

- Resume
- Certifications
- Jobs
- Education
- Networking

Career is broader than employment.

---

## Companion

Preferred.

Represents Military Companion AI.

Avoid:

AI Chat

Chatbot

Assistant

The AI should feel like an integrated companion rather than a separate feature.

---

# Home Screen

## Today's Focus

Preferred.

Represents the single most important recommendation for today.

Avoid:

Tasks

To-do

Checklist

Today's Focus feels supportive rather than demanding.

---

## Opportunities

Preferred.

Represents:

- Discounts
- Financial programs
- Promotions
- Certifications
- Scholarships
- Government support
- Events

Avoid calling everything a "benefit."

Many valuable opportunities are not technically benefits.

---

## Service Progress

Preferred.

Represents overall military progress.

Avoid:

Completion Percentage

Progress Bar

Service Progress provides context.

---

## Next Milestone

Preferred.

Represents:

- Promotion
- Leave
- Training
- Discharge
- Certifications

The word milestone reinforces progress.

---

# Journey Screen

Use:

Current Stage

Next Milestone

Upcoming Opportunities

Service Timeline

Journey Progress

Avoid technical wording.

The Journey screen should feel like navigation through military life.

---

# Notifications

Every notification should include:

1. What happened

2. Why it matters

3. What the soldier should do next

Example:

Promotion registration opens tomorrow.

Register before Friday to remain eligible.

Notifications should guide rather than simply inform.

---

# AI Terminology

The AI should communicate naturally.

Instead of:

"Processing request..."

Use:

"Let me check that for you."

Instead of:

"Error"

Use:

"I couldn't find that information right now."

The Companion should always feel calm and helpful.

---

| Preferred | Avoid |
|-----------|-------|
| Home | Dashboard |
| Soldier | User |
| Journey | Timeline |
| Opportunity | Generic Benefit |
| Today's Focus | Tasks |
| Service Progress | Completion |
| Companion | AI Chat |
| Next Milestone | Deadline |
| Recommendation | Suggestion |
| Guidance | Instructions |

---

# Writing Guidelines

Keep writing:

Short.

Simple.

Encouraging.

Action-oriented.

Explain why something matters.

Never overwhelm the soldier with unnecessary information.

---

# Voice Examples

Instead of:

"You have 27 days of leave."

Say:

"You have 27 days of leave available.

This is a good time to start planning your next trip."

---

Instead of:

"You are eligible."

Say:

"You're now eligible for this opportunity."

---

Instead of:

"Promotion information updated."

Say:

"Your next promotion milestone has been updated."

---

# Final Principle

Military Companion should never sound like a government website.

It should sound like a trusted companion who understands military life and genuinely wants every soldier to succeed.
